## The Problem This Solves

Business and Economics students join Claude Code Club but don't know what to do with it. They can't think of prompts, and blank text boxes are intimidating. The **AI Intern programme** gives them structured missions in realistic business roles. The prompt files below provide **copy-paste starter activities** for discovery sessions and quick demos.

## Running a Session (30-45 mins)

### Setup (5 mins)
- Students open Claude (claude.ai, Claude Desktop, or Claude Code)
- Open [sample-scenarios.md](sample-scenarios.md) and [prompts-business-plan.md](prompts-business-plan.md) side by side

### Activity (20 mins)
1. Students **pick a character** from [sample-scenarios.md](sample-scenarios.md) (Priya, the Mitchells, or Marcus)
2. Students **pick a prompt** from [prompts-business-plan.md](prompts-business-plan.md) — suggest starting with:
   - **Priya + Ramsey Budget Builder** (most accessible)
   - **Priya + JPMorgan Debt Elimination** (satisfying to see the maths)
   - **Marcus + Deloitte Tax Optimisation** (Business Studies students love this one)
3. Students **paste the prompt**, then paste the character's financial details where it says `[DESCRIBE YOUR...]`
4. They read the output — it'll be a full professional-style report

### The Learning Part (10-15 mins)
This is where it gets good. Students should then ask follow-up questions:
- "Explain the debt avalanche method you mentioned — I'm 17 and studying A-level Business"
- "What would change if interest rates went from 4.2% to 6.5%?"
- "How would a recession affect Priya's situation?"
- "Compare this to what would happen if Marcus operated as a sole trader instead"

**Tip:** The follow-up questions are where students start thinking like economists. The initial output is impressive but the conversation is the learning.

### Extension — Make It Their Own
- Students create their own fictional scenario (a character they invent)
- They change numbers in existing scenarios to test "what if" questions
- They try the stock analysis prompts from [prompts-finance.md](prompts-finance.md) with companies they know (Apple, Nike, Tesla)

## Linking to A-Level Specifications

These activities connect naturally to several topics:

**Business Studies:**
- Financial planning and cash flow forecasting
- Sources of finance and debt management
- Business structures (sole trader vs limited company — Marcus scenario)
- Tax and legal obligations
- Investment appraisal

**Economics:**
- Interest rates and monetary policy (change the rates in scenarios)
- Inflation and purchasing power (ask Claude to adjust for inflation)
- Market structures and competition (stock analysis prompts)
- Government taxation policy (Marcus tax optimisation)
- Income distribution and inequality (compare Priya vs Marcus)

## Tips

- Students don't need real financial data — that's what the sample scenarios are for
- If a student wants to use their own family's situation, **discourage this** — use fictional data only
- The prompts use American financial terminology (401K, IRA, etc.) — ask Claude to "convert this advice to the UK context" as a follow-up
- Some prompts reference US-specific products — this is actually a good teaching moment about different financial systems
- If students finish early, point them to [economics-resources.md](economics-resources.md) for the GitHub repo of economist AI skills
