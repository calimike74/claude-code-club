# Sample Financial Scenarios for Claude Prompts

Use these fictional characters with any of the prompts in [prompts-business-plan.md](prompts-business-plan.md) or [prompts-finance.md](prompts-finance.md). Copy the scenario, paste it into the bracketed section at the end of a prompt, and see what Claude produces.

---

## Scenario 1: Recent Graduate — "Priya Sharma"

**Best with:** Ramsey Budget Builder, Goldman Sachs Wealth Diagnostic, JPMorgan Debt Elimination

```
My name is Priya Sharma. I'm 23, single, living in Manchester in a rented flat (£750/month).

Income: £28,000 salary as a junior marketing executive (£1,950/month take-home)

Monthly expenses:
- Rent: £750
- Council tax: £95
- Utilities (gas, electric, water): £120
- Phone contract: £35
- Groceries: £200
- Transport (bus pass): £65
- Subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, gym): £55
- Socialising/eating out: £200
- Clothes/personal: £80

Debts:
- Student loan: £42,000 (Plan 2, repaying automatically through salary)
- Credit card: £1,800 at 22.9% APR (minimum payment £45/month)
- Buy now pay later: £350 remaining on a laptop (0% for 6 more months)

Savings: £620 in a savings account earning 1.2%
Investments: None
Insurance: None beyond employer's basic pension (3% matched to 5%)

Goals: Clear the credit card, build an emergency fund, start investing,
eventually save for a house deposit. No idea where to start.
```

## Scenario 2: Young Family — "James and Sarah Mitchell"

**Best with:** Vanguard Retirement Planner, Charles Schwab Emergency Fund, Fidelity College Savings, Northwestern Mutual Insurance Audit

```
We are James (34) and Sarah (32) Mitchell, married, living in Bristol.
We have two children: Olivia (4) and Noah (18 months).

Combined income: £72,000 (James: £45,000 as an IT project manager,
Sarah: £27,000 part-time as a teaching assistant)

Monthly take-home combined: £4,350

Monthly expenses:
- Mortgage: £1,100 (£195,000 remaining, 25-year term, 4.2% fixed for 2 more years)
- Childcare (nursery for Noah, pre-school for Olivia): £1,400
- Council tax: £165
- Utilities: £180
- Groceries: £450
- Car finance (Kia): £280/month (£8,400 remaining, 2 years left)
- Petrol: £120
- Car insurance: £85
- Phone contracts x2: £70
- Subscriptions: £45
- Children's activities/clothes: £150
- Family outings: £100

Debts:
- Mortgage: £195,000 at 4.2%
- Car finance: £8,400 at 6.9% APR
- James's student loan: £18,000 remaining (Plan 1)
- Sarah's student loan: £12,000 remaining (Plan 1)

Savings: £3,200 in an easy-access ISA
Investments: James has a workplace pension worth £22,000 (contributes 5%, employer matches 3%)
Insurance: Basic life cover through James's employer (2x salary)

Goals: Build a 3-month emergency fund, start saving for the children's
university costs, pay off the car early, and work out whether Sarah should
increase her pension contributions. Worried about what happens financially
if James can't work.
```

## Scenario 3: Small Business Owner — "Marcus Williams"

**Best with:** Deloitte Tax Optimisation, Morgan Stanley Portfolio Architect, BlackRock Lifetime Roadmap

```
I'm Marcus Williams, 41, divorced, one child (daughter, 12, lives with
her mum — I pay £600/month child maintenance).

I run a small graphic design agency in Leeds with 3 employees.
The business is a limited company.

Personal income:
- Director's salary: £12,570/year (tax-free allowance)
- Dividends from company: £40,000/year
- Total personal income: £52,570

Business financials:
- Annual turnover: £185,000
- Annual costs (salaries, rent, software, equipment): £118,000
- Corporation tax paid: approximately £12,700
- Business account balance: £28,000
- No business debt

Personal monthly expenses:
- Rent (flat): £850
- Child maintenance: £600
- Utilities: £110
- Car (owned outright, 2019 BMW): insurance £95, fuel £150
- Food: £280
- Phone/internet: £60
- Gym and social: £120
- Personal subscriptions: £30

Personal finances:
- Current account: £8,500
- Stocks & Shares ISA: £15,000 (mix of index funds)
- Workplace pension from previous employment: £34,000 (not contributing)
- SIPP: £0
- No personal debt other than Plan 2 student loan (£24,000 remaining)

Goals: Work out the most tax-efficient way to take money from the company,
start a SIPP, build investments to £200,000 by age 50, and ensure my
daughter is financially supported through university.
```

## Scenario 4: Stock Analysis Practice

**Best with:** Prompts from [prompts-finance.md](prompts-finance.md)

Students don't need fake data for these — they pick real companies they know. Here are some starter suggestions:

| Prompt | Try with |
|--------|----------|
| Full Stock Analysis | Apple (AAPL) or Tesla (TSLA) — companies students know |
| Compare Two Stocks | Nike (NKE) vs Adidas (ADDYY) — relatable brands |
| Build a Portfolio | "I have £5,000 to invest with a growth strategy and 10-year horizon" |
| Assess Risk | Meta (META) — good for discussing regulation, competition, AI spend |
| Understand Earnings | Google "Apple Q1 2026 earnings" and paste the summary |

**Tip for students:** After Claude analyses a stock, ask follow-up questions like:
- "How would a recession affect this company?"
- "What would Warren Buffett think of this stock?"
- "Explain the P/E ratio you mentioned — I'm an A-level Business student"

## How to Use These in Club

1. **Pick a scenario** that interests you
2. **Pick a prompt** from [prompts-business-plan.md](prompts-business-plan.md)
3. **Paste the prompt** into Claude, then paste the scenario where it says `[DESCRIBE YOUR...]`
4. **Read the output** — it will be a detailed professional-style report
5. **Ask follow-up questions** — "Explain X in simpler terms" or "What would change if interest rates went up?"
6. **Try changing the numbers** — what happens if Priya gets a £5,000 pay rise? What if Marcus loses a client worth £40,000?

The real learning happens in step 5 and 6 — that's where you start thinking like an economist.
