
Economists: I’ve launched a small project to make AI coding tools actually useful for your day‑to‑day work.  

Awesome Econ AI Stuff – a growing collection of reusable AI “skills” for Claude Code, Cursor and Gemini CLI, tailored to empirical + theoretical economics

The idea is simple: instead of prompting from scratch every time, we encode best‑practice workflows as portable skills: 

- Data cleaning and exploratory data analysis for typical economic datasets 
- Reproducible project protocols 
- Simulation and numerical methods 
- Robustness checks 
- Writing and presentation processes

All written in a format your AI tools can load and follow consistently.

If you use Claude Code, Cursor, or Gemini CLI, you can drop these skills into your projects and get an assistant that “thinks like an economist”: 

- Understands panels, diff‑in‑diff, IV, RCTs, networks, etc. 
- Respects your coding style and file structure 
- Helps with documentation and teaching materials 

Customisable as you want. Not generic software‑engineering prompts, but rather something targeted to what economists need day to day.
