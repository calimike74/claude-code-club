## Your New Brief

Sarah Chen forwards you an email from the CEO:

> **From:** Tom Aspinall, CEO
> **To:** Sarah Chen, CFO
> **Subject:** Board question — hiring plan
>
> Sarah,
>
> Before Thursday's board meeting, James (our tech investor) emailed me asking whether we can afford to hire a second senior developer in Q3. He thinks we need to accelerate the product roadmap to win enterprise deals faster.
>
> I'm inclined to agree — but I need the numbers. A senior developer costs £65,000/year (about £5,400/month fully loaded with NI and pension). The argument is that faster product development converts the two enterprise prospects sooner — potentially pulling them from month 5 to month 3.
>
> Can your team model this? I need to know:
> 1. Can we afford it in any of the three scenarios?
> 2. If not, how much additional funding would we need to cover the gap?
> 3. Is it worth it — does the accelerated enterprise revenue outweigh the cost?
>
> Need this by end of day Wednesday.
>
> Tom

---

## What You Need to Produce

Using Claude Code and the financial data from this module:

1. **A revised forecast** — add the senior developer hire from month 3 across all three scenarios
2. **The trade-off analysis** — does pulling enterprise revenue forward to month 3 (instead of month 5) offset the additional salary cost?
3. **A one-paragraph recommendation** to the CEO — hire now, hire later, or don't hire? Defend it with numbers.

---

## Success Criteria

Your output is strong if:

- [ ] The revised forecast clearly shows the cash impact of the hire in each scenario
- [ ] You've calculated the net effect (additional cost vs. accelerated revenue)
- [ ] Your recommendation is specific — not "it depends" but "hire in month X because..."
- [ ] You've identified the break-even point for the hiring decision itself
- [ ] A non-financial person (the CEO) could read your recommendation and make a decision
